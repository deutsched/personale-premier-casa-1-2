The Accademia di Santa Cecilia directed at La Scala in Milan

Over 800 guests attending the exhibition of the orchestra directed by Antonio Pappano during the concert promoted by FAI and Deutsche.

The concert

23rd of October ? Milan


Great success at La Scala Theater in Milan for the eight edition of  Deutsche's concert  in favor of FAI - Fondo Ambiente Italiano (the National Trust). This year one of the most important Italian artistic directors, Antonio Pappano, together with the Accademia di Santa Cecilia (Rome) performed a concert from Ludwing Van Beethoven and Camille Saint- Saëns. Extraordinary exhibition of the Barbiere of Siviglia of Gioachino Rossini at the end of the event that has generated enthusiastic applause from the audience of over 700 guests from the financial, corporate and institutional environment.

The event is part of the cycle 'Major International Symphony Orchestras' of Deutsche. Thanks to this initiative some of the world's most important orchestras have performed at La Scala: the London Symphony Orchestra and Daniel Harding (2009), the Bamberger Symphoniker with Georges Prêtre (2010), the Münchner Philharmoniker with Christoph Eschenbach (2011), the Philharmonia Orchestra with Esa Pekka Salonen (2012),  the Sächsische Staatskapelle Dresden conducted by Myung- Whun Chung with the special participation of the pianist Daniil Trifonov (2013) The Orchestre National de France concucted by Daniele Gatti (2014) and last year with the Cleveland Orchestra and Radu Lapu.

The project is part of the initiatives in favor of the Italian heritage of art and culture: all the revenues of the concert will be donated to the FAI. Through its Foundation, Deutsche stands alongside FAI to support and  protect our naturalistic and artistic heritage.
