Partner

Associazione Italiana Sindrome di Williams

www.aisw.it/

Associazione Italiana Sindrome Di Williams Onlus was born in 1996 at the initiative of a group of parents, with the aim of helping families get a correct information on clinic, rehab, educatuion, social and legal aspects regarding the Williams' Syndrome.

Club Itaca Rome

www.progettoitaca.org

Itaca Project Rome is a voluntary association founded in 2010 to offer free services to people affected by mental diseases and their families. The Association was inspired to Itaca Project Milan, founded in 1999, whose know-how, operational model and functional system were adopted in Rome.

Fondazione Cometa

www.puntocometa.org/

Cometa is a reality of many families involved in the welcoming, education and training of children and young people and in the support of their families. Young people are introduced to reality and they are trained in order to catch the meaning and the beauty of life in the simple sharing of the every-day life and where families are helped in their educational path. From this experience Cometa Training and the Oliver Twist School have born, in order to answer to the need of a professional education with new paradigms, and a new connection with the working environment.

FAI - Fondo per l'Ambiente Italiano

www.fondoambiente.it

The Fondo per l'Ambiente Italiano (FAI) is the National Trust of Italy.The organisation was established in 1975 on the model of the British National Trust. It is a private non-profit organisation. Its purpose is to protect elements of Italy's physical heritage which might otherwise be lost.

Fondazione Hospice Seràgnoli

www.fondhs.org

Fondazione Hospice MT. C. Seràgnoli Onlus takes care of palliative medication Cure Palliative with the aim of responding to a community need. The Foundation's main objective is to create an innovative and excellent model to support  patients with incurable diseases. Beside assistive activities, the foundation promotes research and training programs at a national level, in cooperation with scientific institutions.

Fondazione Theodora Onlus

it.theodora.org

The Foundation began its activity in 1995. Today it is present in 18 hospitals in 11 cities with 28 Italian Giggle Doctors who visit and bring a smile each year to more than 35,000 children in care . Theodora Foundation selects the giggle doctors, finances their training, seminars update and their continuous psychological counceling, as well as organises their activities, ensuring high quality visits. Giggle doctors are completely free for hospitals and, of course, for young patients' families.

Portofranco Milano

www.portofranco.org/

Portofranco is a center of study assistance for middle and high school students. In 2016 it hosted more than 1.700 students thereof 500 foreigners/immigrants. Thanks to more than 350 volunteers, among current and retired teachers, professionals and university students, Portofranco offers free support to daily homework, remedial programs and behavioral issues totaling about 20.000 hours per year.

San Patrignano

www.sanpatrignano.org

The Community of San Patrignano is the most important anti-drug centre in all of Europe and it houses over 1,300 young people. From 1978 to the present day, San Patrignano has welcomed more than 25,000 people from hundreds of different nations, totally free of charge, without charges for either their families or the State, offering them a home, health and legal assistance, the possibility to study, learn a trade and return fully to society at the end of their recovery path.
