2. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files which the websites the user visits send to the user's terminal equipment (usually to the browser), where they are stored in memory and then sent back to the same websites if the user visits them again. When navigating on a website, users may also receive cookies from different websites or web servers on their terminal equipment (so-called "third party cookies"). This happens because the website visited may contain components such as, for example, images, maps, audio or specific links to web pages in other domains which are housed on servers other than the one on which the page requested is located. There are two different types of cookies:

  • Technical and aggregated statistical cookies which do not require the user's consent for installation on their terminal equipment;
  • Cookies which are used for non-strictly technical or statistical purposes; these do require the user's consent for their use.
3. What type of cookies are used on Deutsche S.p.A. websites?

All our websites, including the portals providing access to areas reserved to customers, commercial partners and operators use technical cookies. These cookies are necessary for two reasons:

  1. To carry out activities strictly necessary for website functions: the cookies are used to store the user's session and carry out other activities strictly necessary for use of the websites, for example concerning the distribution of traffic.
  2. To save preferences, for optimisation and statistical purposes: in this case the cookies are used to save the navigation preferences and optimise the user's navigation experience. These cookies include, for example, those used to select the language or currency or to manage statistics by the website owner.

5. How to deny consent for use of cookies?

A user who continues navigating on our websites (closing the information banner or accessing any content on the underlying page) accepts the use of any cookies which may be installed by the website on their terminal equipment. All users may, however, at any time deny access and block use of cookies:

  1. The User may exercise the right to block tracking according to information given in the privacy policy of the third party, using the opt-out link if expressly provided, or by directly contacting the third-party operator;
  2. Or by changing their own browser settings.

Please note that full deactivation of technical cookies by the user may compromise the use of various functions on our websites and, in some cases, result in complete impossibility of navigating on the websites. However, disabling non-technical cookies does not prejudice use of our websites. Below, we provide instructions for the management of cookies with reference to the main browsers in use.

7. Definitions

Personal data (or Data)
Personal data is any information concerning an individual who is identified or identifiable, including indirectly, through reference to any other information, including a personal identification number.

Use data
This is personal data collected automatically by the Application (or by the third party applications the application uses) including: IP addresses or domain names of the computers used by the User connecting to the Application, the URI (Uniform Resource Identifier) addresses, the time of the request, the method used to submit the request to the server, the size of the file sent in reply, the numerical code indicating the response status of the server (successful, error, etc.) the Country of origin, the characteristics of the browser and of the operating system used by the visitor, the various timings of the visit (for example the time spent on each page) and details of the itinerary followed within the Application, with particular reference to the sequence of pages consulted, the parameters of the operating system and the User's information technology environment.

User
The individual using the website who must be the same as the Data Subject or be authorised by the person whose Personal Data are the subject of processing.

Data Subject
The natural or legal person to whom the Personal Data refer.

Data Controller (or Controller)
The natural or legal person, the public administration and any other entity, association or organisation which, including together with other controllers, makes decisions concerning the purposes and the methods for processing personal data and the instruments used, including the security profile, for the functioning and use of the website. The Data Controller, unless otherwise specified, is the owner of the website.
